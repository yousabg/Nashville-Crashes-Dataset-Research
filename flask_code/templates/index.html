Exploring the Causes of Traffic Accidents

A Comprehensive Analysis Leveraging Data Science Techniques

Project Overview

This project delves into the multifaceted reasons behind traffic accidents in the U.S., utilizing the extensive Fatality Analysis Reporting System (FARS) dataset and a detailed dataset from Nashville. Through rigorous data analysis, we aim to uncover patterns and factors contributing to accidents, from common causes like drunk driving and speed, to less apparent factors such as demographic differences.

Objectives

  • Analyze nationwide accident data to identify major causes of traffic fatalities.
  • Conduct a focused study on Nashville's accident data to pinpoint local issues.
  • Utilize data science methodologies to filter, analyze, and interpret complex datasets.

Key Areas of Analysis

The project examines various factors that might influence accident rates, including:

  • Drunk Driving
  • Speed Limit Adherence and Travel Speed
  • Demographic Variables (Sex, Age)

Datasets and Tools

Analysis is based on the FARS dataset managed by NHTSA and the Nashville Traffic Accidents dataset provided by the Metro Nashville Police Department. The project employs various data science tools and techniques for data cleaning, analysis, and visualization to ensure accurate and insightful findings.
